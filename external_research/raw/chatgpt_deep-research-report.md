## Methodology and limits

This build follows the requested ordering: source discovery → Window A major anchors → Window B incident/violation layer → obscure watchlist → join logic. The ceasefire boundary (effective 27 Nov 2024) is treated as a factual segmentation marker based on reporting that the ceasefire took effect at 4am local time. citeturn17view2

Event extraction rules used here: each row is source-backed via `source_url`, and details not explicitly present in the source are left blank or described as “reported” to avoid overclaiming. Where sources provide a multi-incident daily roundup (common in local outlets), the current dataset sometimes preserves that as a single “bundle row” to stay within size constraints; in subsequent expansions, those should be split into atomic incidents (one incident per row) while retaining the same `source_url`.

Known collection limitations in this pass: (a) some outlets are partially paywalled or intermittently inaccessible; (b) several outlets publish fast “latest news” items that later update without stable text snapshots; (c) this deliverable is a starter registry emphasizing precision and joinability over exhaustive day-by-day completeness for all 2024‑11‑27 → 2026‑03‑14 dates. For full daily completeness, the best next step is systematic scraping/archival capture of NNA daily security briefs and LAF statements, then normalizing into one-row-per-incident.

Local subreddit archive context files were provided for correlation work; they inform the “priority_windows” section but do not constitute proof of coordination. fileciteturn0file0 fileciteturn0file1
