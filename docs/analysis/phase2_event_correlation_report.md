# Phase 2 Event Correlation Report

## Scope
This memo combines the local archive findings with source-backed external events. It is a first-pass investigative aid, not a final verdict. The goal is to identify where subreddit activity appears to move in step with real-world escalation, diplomacy, or identity-charged news.

## Important correction on flair
The visible flair system in the archive is broader than a simple `Lebanese` versus `Israeli` split.
Observed high-frequency labels include:
- `Israeli`
- `Lebanese`
- `Diaspora Lebanese`
- `Diaspora Jew`
- `Diaspora Israeli`
- `Palestinian`
- `Non-Canaanite`
- `Iraqi`
- `Israeli Arab`
- large volumes of `Unflaired/Unknown`

That matters because flair is useful, but it cannot be treated as a binary or as verified identity.

## Strongest correlations

### 2020-08: Beirut blast + aid/normalization framing
Local signal:
- `125` posts and `1420` comments in August 2020.
- Top threads included Beirut-blast sympathy, Israeli aid offers, and explicit comparisons to the UAE-Israel normalization deal.
- The local topic mix is not just grief or solidarity; it quickly turns toward normalization discourse.

Interpretation:
- This is an early example of an external humanitarian shock being used in the subreddit as an opening for political or normalization messaging.
- That does not prove coordination, but it is a recurring pattern worth testing later across multiple event windows.

External sources:
- Beirut explosion coverage: [AP / Beirut blast overview](https://www.ap.org/news-highlights/best-of-the-week/2020/massive-explosion-tears-through-lebanese-capital-beirut/)

- Aid and diplomatic sensitivity summary: [2020 Beirut explosion background](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2020_Beirut_explosion)

### 2021-05: Gaza war spillover + Lebanon rocket fire
Local signal:
- `133` posts and `1937` comments in May 2021.
- High-performing threads revolve around safety, translation, anti-Hamas framing, and accusations of anti-Semitic propaganda.
- The month looks less like ordinary cross-cultural chat and more like conflict-era discourse imported from a regional war cycle.

Interpretation:
- The subreddit appears to shift quickly from peacetime identity performance to war-adjacent messaging when Israel-Palestine violence intensifies.
- That should be tested quantitatively by comparing conflict months versus calmer months for word choices and flair participation.

External sources:
- Rockets fired from southern Lebanon during the May 2021 crisis: [AP mirror](https://www.wmdt.com/i/lebanese-security-officials-say-several-rockets-fired-from-south-lebanon-toward-israel/)

### 2023-10 to 2023-11: Oct. 7 aftermath + Hezbollah opens the northern front
Local signal:
- October 2023: `129` posts, `2361` comments.
- November 2023: `130` posts, `2975` comments.
- Top October threads are highly reactive to Hamas, Hezbollah, and atrocity narratives.
- Top November threads shift into identity policing and anti-Hezbollah discourse, including posts about the Lebanon subreddit being overrun by Hezbollah supporters.

Interpretation:
- This is a major narrative inflection point. The archive shows a rapid move into polarizing war framing, identity boundary-setting, and anti-Hezbollah positioning.
- This is a high-priority window for deeper linguistic review and account-cluster analysis.

External sources:

### 2024-06: major border escalation and war fears
Local signal:
- `134` posts and `3893` comments in June 2024.
- Top threads mix food, sentiment, and direct meta-conflict about what the subreddit is becoming.
- One of the strongest local signs is not only external escalation, but internal strain: “What happened to the sub?” and calls to remove inflammatory posters.
- Flair breakdown that month shows `Israeli` comments (`844`) materially outnumber `Lebanese` comments (`183`), with `Diaspora Lebanese` also active (`340`).

Interpretation:
- June 2024 looks like a bridge period where the subreddit's own legitimacy becomes contested from inside, not just from critics outside it.
- This is important because coordination, if present, may show up as meta-thread steering and norm-setting, not only overt propaganda.

External sources:
- Killing of senior Hezbollah commander Taleb Abdullah and subsequent escalation: [AP](https://apnews.com/article/a404a607c3f96850d0dde1a5cc70c41b)

### 2024-09 to 2024-10: the clearest event-synchronization window
Local signal:
- September 2024 is the archive peak: `293` posts and `6634` comments.
- October 2024 remains near-peak: `197` posts and `6509` comments.
- Thread titles in late September include overt celebration around the killing of Hezbollah figures and highly emotional conflict reactions.
- At the same time, the archive also contains first-person harm reports like `My house got fucking bombed`, which means the same event window includes both celebratory and direct-victim perspectives.
- Flair activity in September and October is especially notable:
  - September 2024 comments: `Israeli 1564`, `Lebanese 657`, `Diaspora Lebanese 303`
  - October 2024 comments: `Israeli 1164`, `Lebanese 779`, `Palestinian 98`, `Diaspora Israeli 191`

Interpretation:
- This is the strongest candidate window for demonstrating event-coupled narrative behavior.
- The volume spike aligns not only with war escalation, but with a sharp tonal split: celebration, grief, identity claims, and attempts to define who speaks for Lebanon.
- If you want one initial “core case study” for the final website, September-October 2024 is the best candidate.

Interpretation:

- If the same accounts dominate both war spikes and normalization/diplomacy spikes, that continuity may be analytically important.

## What stands out so far
- The archive is highly event-reactive. The biggest activity spikes align with real periods of war escalation, not random community growth.
- Several spike windows are not only reactive; they are identity-reactive. Lebanese, Israeli, diaspora, and unlabeled participants appear in different proportions across different crises.
- The strongest substantive lead is not “Hebrew means smoking gun.” It is the combination of:
  - event timing
  - tone shifts
  - recurrent high-volume accounts
  - self-presented identity labels
  - meta-threads trying to define who belongs and what the subreddit is for
- September-October 2024 is the best first deep-dive window for the final case study.

## Recommended next analytical passes
1. Compare the top 50 accounts across the major spike windows and measure continuity.
2. Segment timeline charts by flair rather than only by total volume.
3. Pull the full Hebrew subset into a separate review file and compare its tone to English posts in the same windows.
4. Identify accounts that present as Lebanese but show unusual timing, vocabulary, or rhetorical patterns.
5. Build thread-network views for September-October 2024 and March 2025.

## Caution
None of the above proves state coordination by itself. What it does show is a measurable pattern of event-synchronized discourse, repeated identity framing, and a subreddit whose peak periods track major regional escalations very closely. That is enough to justify a much deeper second pass.
